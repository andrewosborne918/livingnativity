Station 2: The Prophet Isaiah

Click "play" for audio.

As you turn right around the corner, a different sight comes into view. There are no Christmas lights, no familiar markings of Christmas. There is only a man speaking. His name is Isaiah. He was God’s spokesman, called a prophet. He spoke on God’s behalf. To us this type of speaking seems unusual. To the people of God, it was a blessing. In order for them to trust that the prophet spoke for God, his words need to come true. (To learn more, read Deuteronomy 18:20-22 in the Bible)

One of the most famous things that Isaiah said was, “The virgin will conceive and give birth to a son and will call his name ‘Immanuel’ (Isaiah 7:14). Immanuel means ‘God is with us.’ Isaiah was talking to the disobedient King of Israel and telling him that God would come and be with his people. This was a promise, a promise to literally be together with his people in spite of the ways a King was not for His people.
